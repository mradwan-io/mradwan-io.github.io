Teaching & Supervision

4 courses at Cairo University · MSc & PhD student supervision

I teach undergraduate and graduate courses in electronics, IC design, and high-speed interfaces at Cairo University's Faculty of Engineering. My courses are directly informed by my doctoral research at OSU and industrial experience at Intel and Vidatronic.

Courses

Current Teaching

EEC 301 · 3 CREDIT HOURS
Electronics I
Undergraduate · Fall Semester

Foundations of modern electronics: semiconductor physics, p-n junctions, diode circuits, BJT and MOSFET device physics, biasing, and small-signal models. Concludes with single-stage amplifier design and frequency response.

Semiconductor PhysicsBJT & MOSFETAmplifiersSPICE Lab
Prerequisites: Circuit Analysis, Physics IIFall
EEC 415 · 3 CREDIT HOURS
Analog Integrated Circuits
Undergraduate · Spring Semester

CMOS analog circuit design: differential pairs, current mirrors, op-amps, feedback, frequency compensation, comparators, bandgap references, and an introduction to data converters. IC design flow using Cadence Virtuoso.

CMOS CircuitsOp-AmpsBandgapCadence Virtuoso
Prerequisites: Electronics I & IISpring
EEC 501 · 3 CREDIT HOURS
High-Speed I/O Interface Design
Graduate · Fall Semester

Complete treatment of high-speed serial link design: channel modeling, transmitter pre-emphasis (FFE), continuous-time linear equalization (CTLE), decision feedback equalization (DFE), clock and data recovery (CDR), jitter analysis, and signal integrity for 10–112 Gbps links. Directly based on doctoral research at OSU.

SerDesDFECDRSignal IntegrityPAM4
Prerequisites: Analog IC Design, Digital CommsFall
EEC 510 · 3 CREDIT HOURS
Power Management IC Design
Graduate · Spring Semester

Comprehensive treatment of integrated power management: LDO voltage regulators (topology, PSR, transient response), switching DC-DC converters (buck/boost/SEPIC), control loop design, power integrity for SoC and IoT. Draws directly on Vidatronic industrial experience and Intel Labs LDO internship.

LDODC-DCNB-IoTControl Loops
Prerequisites: Analog IC DesignSpring
Supervision

Graduate Students

🎓 MSc Supervision

I supervise MSc students working on topics within high-speed IC design and power management. Prospective students should have a strong background in analog circuits or digital communications.

  • SerDes receiver architectures and equalization
  • Low-power DC-DC converter design
  • Mixed-signal circuit design for IoT
  • CMOS LDO and reference circuit design

🔬 PhD Supervision

PhD positions may be available for highly motivated candidates with exceptional academic records. Research directions are aligned with my active research program in high-speed wireline and power management.

  • Speculative DFE structures beyond PAM4
  • Energy-proportional serial link architectures
  • Scalable power management for AI hardware
  • TIA and receiver circuits for optical links

To enquire about graduate research opportunities, please email a brief description of your background and interests.
