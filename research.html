Research & Publications

High-speed wireline receivers · Power management ICs · 5 peer-reviewed papers

My research focuses on two interconnected themes: (1) high-speed serial link architectures — particularly speculative DFE structures that achieve linear hardware complexity for PAM-N signaling — and (2) energy-efficient power management ICs for low-power IoT and SoC applications.

Core Areas

Research Focus

⚡
High-Speed Wireline Receivers

SerDes architecture, CTLE/FFE/DFE equalization, CDR circuits for 10–112 Gbps applications. PhD thesis developed novel multi-stage speculative DFE with linear complexity for PAM-N signaling at The Ohio State University.

DFESerDesPAM4CDR
🔋
Power Management IC Design

LDO regulators, DC-DC buck/boost converters, reference circuits, and switched-capacitor topologies. MSc thesis developed a low-ripple hybrid DC-DC converter for NB-IoT. Intel Labs internship: high-PSR capacitor-less LDO.

LDODC-DCNB-IoT
🔬
Mixed-Signal IC Design

Bridging analog and digital design domains: TIA circuits for optical receivers, mixed-mode LDOs, PCB design, and Cadence Virtuoso-based IC design methodology used in both research and 3+ years of industrial IP development.
