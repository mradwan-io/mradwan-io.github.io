About

Research & Background

IC designer, educator, and PhD graduate of The Ohio State University.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Electrical Communications at Cairo University's Faculty of Engineering, where I joined in July 2025 after completing my PhD at The Ohio State University.

My doctoral research at OSU (2020–2024) focused on novel Decision Feedback Equalizer (DFE) structures for wireline receivers. I developed reconfigurable multi-stage, multi-standard PAM-N DFEs performing multi-tap speculation with linear (rather than exponential) hardware complexity — enabling high-speed I/O interfaces that are both fast and energy-proportional.

My MSc research at Cairo University (2017–2020) focused on power management IC design — specifically, a low-ripple hybrid DC-DC buck converter for NB-IoT applications. My BSc graduation project (2016) was a low-power programmable frequency divider for a sub-GHz integer PLL.

In industry, I interned at Intel Labs (Summer 2022), designing a scalable high-PSR capacitor-less mixed-mode LDO. Before returning to academia, I spent over three years as an IC Design Engineer at Vidatronic Inc., developing power management IPs including reference circuits, LDOs, and DC-DC buck/boost converters.
