About

Research & Background

IC designer, educator, and PhD graduate of The Ohio State University.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Electrical Communications at Cairo University's Faculty of Engineering, where I joined in July 2025 after completing my PhD at The Ohio State University.

My doctoral research at OSU (2020–2024) focused on novel Decision Feedback Equalizer (DFE) structures for wireline and optical receivers. I developed reconfigurable multi-stage PAM-N DFEs with multi-tap speculation at linear (not exponential) hardware complexity, enabling energy-proportional high-speed data transfer.

My MSc at Cairo University (2017–2020) covered power management IC design — specifically a low-ripple hybrid switched-capacitor DC-DC buck converter for NB-IoT. My BSc project (2016) was a low-power programmable frequency divider for a sub-GHz integer PLL.

In industry, I interned at Intel Labs (Summer 2022), designing a scalable high-PSR capacitor-less mixed-mode LDO. Before that, I spent 3+ years as an IC Design Engineer at Vidatronic Inc. developing production power management IPs: LDOs, reference circuits, and DC-DC converters.
